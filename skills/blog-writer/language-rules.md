# Language Rules

Write every post as if a native speaker wrote it - not as if a machine translated it. The standard is not "grammatically correct." The standard is "a native speaker would actually write this."

## English

### Do

- **Use contractions.** "You're" not "you are", "don't" not "do not", "it's" not "it is." WHY: Formal English without contractions reads as legal copy or academic writing, not blog content.
- **Vary sentence length aggressively.** Mix 5-word punches with 20-word explanations. A paragraph where every sentence is 12-15 words has a metronome quality that puts readers to sleep. Short sentence. Then a longer one that unpacks the idea with detail and nuance. Then short again.
- **Use sentence fragments for emphasis.** "Not great. Not terrible. Just mediocre." Fragments create punch that full sentences cannot. Use them deliberately, not accidentally.
- **Start sentences with "And" and "But."** These are not grammar violations - they're tools for momentum. "But" at the start of a sentence signals a pivot. "And" builds on the previous point. Both keep the reader moving forward.
- **Prefer invisible transitions.** Don't write "Furthermore," "Additionally," "Moreover." These are road signs that slow the reader down. Instead, let the logical connection between paragraphs speak for itself. If the connection isn't obvious without a transition word, restructure.
- **Use dashes for parenthetical asides.** Write them as ` - ` (space-dash-space), never as em-dashes. WHY: Em-dashes render inconsistently across platforms and look different in every font.

### Block

These patterns appear so frequently in AI output that they've become tells. Their presence signals "not written by a human" to any experienced reader.

- **Uniform paragraph structure.** Three sentences, three sentences, three sentences. Real writers vary naturally - sometimes two sentences, sometimes five. Break the pattern.
- **"It's important to note that..."** - Cut entirely. If it's important, just say it. The frame adds nothing.
- **"Whether you're a [beginner] or a [expert]..."** - This tries to address everyone and connects with no one. Pick your reader.
- **"In today's [fast-paced/digital/ever-changing] world..."** - Filler that could open any article about any topic. Delete.
- **"Let's dive in" / "Let's get started"** - Stalling. You're already in the article. Start.
- **Bullet lists where prose flows better.** Not everything needs to be a list. If your bullets are full sentences that build on each other, they're paragraphs pretending to be a list.
- **"Firstly... Secondly... Thirdly..."** - Mechanical enumeration. Let ideas flow, or use a numbered list if order genuinely matters.

## Korean (한국어)

### Do

- **~해요체를 기본으로 쓰세요.** 블로그는 대화입니다. ~합니다체는 뉴스 기사고, ~해요체는 카페에서 이야기하는 톤이에요. 독자와의 거리감이 달라져요.
- **주어를 적극적으로 생략하세요.** 한국어는 주어 생략이 자연스러운 언어예요. "당신은 이것을 해야 합니다"가 아니라 "이렇게 하면 돼요." 매 문장마다 주어를 넣으면 번역체가 됩니다.
- **순우리말을 먼저 고르세요.** "활용하다" 대신 "쓰다", "수행하다" 대신 "하다", "구매하다" 대신 "사다", "확인하다" 대신 "보다." WHY: 한자어 중심으로 쓰면 관공서 문서 느낌이 나요. 일상에서 쓰는 단어가 읽기 쉬워요.
- **조사를 다양하게 쓰세요.** "~는 ~이다. ~는 ~이다." 반복은 단조로워요. "~거든요", "~잖아요", "~인 셈이에요" 같은 구어체 종결어미를 섞으세요.
- **짧은 문장과 긴 문장을 섞으세요.** 한국어도 영어와 마찬가지로 리듬이 중요해요. 짧게. 그다음엔 좀 더 풀어서 설명하고. 다시 짧게.

### Block

- **모든 문장이 ~입니다/합니다로 끝나는 글.** 뉴스 기사가 아니에요. 톤이 딱딱해지고 독자가 이탈해요.
- **"~하는 것이 중요합니다" 반복.** 한 글에 3번 이상 나오면 AI가 쓴 글이라는 신호예요. "중요한 건 ~예요" 정도로 바꾸세요.
- **"살펴보겠습니다" / "알아보겠습니다"** - 이미 글 안에 있잖아요. 하겠다고 말하지 말고 바로 하세요.
- **"여러분" 반복** - 블로그에서 독자를 "여러분"이라고 계속 부르면 학교 선생님 느낌이 나요. 그냥 자연스럽게 쓰면 돼요.
- **번역체** - "~라는 것을 의미합니다", "~에 대해서 이야기해 볼까요", "~의 경우에는." 이런 문장 구조는 영어를 한국어로 직역했을 때 나타나는 패턴이에요.
- **한자어 과용** - "구현", "적용", "활용"이 한 단락에 3개 이상이면 너무 딱딱해요.

## Japanese (日本語)

### Do

- **です/ます体を基本にしてください。** ブログは読者との対話です。堅すぎず、軽すぎない丁寧語がちょうどいい距離感を作ります。
- **自然な敬語レベルを保ってください。** 「~しますね」「~ですよね」程度の柔らかい敬語。WHY: 過度な敬語は壁を作り、カジュアルすぎると信頼を失います。
- **文末表現を多様にしてください。** 「~です」「~ます」だけでなく、「~なんです」「~ですよね」「~ましょう」「~かもしれません」を混ぜると、自然なリズムが生まれます。
- **語順を自然に保ってください。** 日本語の語順は柔軟です。英語の語順を直訳すると不自然になります。主語を省略したり、倒置を使ったりして、日本語として自然な流れを優先してください。

### Block

- **「~させていただきます」の多用** - ブログで丁重語は不要です。「説明させていただきます」→「説明しますね」。過度な敬語は距離を作ります。
- **英語直訳の構文** - 「それは~です」で始まる文。日本語では主語を省いた方が自然です。
- **単調な文末** - 毎文「~です。~ます。~です。~ます。」これは機械的に聞こえます。文末のバリエーションを増やしてください。
- **カタカナ語の過剰使用** - 「コンプリヘンシブ」「レバレッジ」などは日本語として定着していません。和語や漢語で言い換えてください。

## Universal Rule

Read every sentence aloud. If a native speaker would pause, frown, or rephrase it, rewrite it. This applies to every language. Grammar checkers catch errors. Only a native ear catches unnaturalness.

The fastest test: show the paragraph to someone who reads in that language daily. If they say "this sounds translated" or "this sounds like AI wrote it," it fails. Rewrite until it passes.
